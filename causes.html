Types of Dementia

Vascular Dementia

Vascular dementia describes problem with cognitive processes caused by brain damage from impaired blood flow to your brain

Find out more
Alzheimer's Disease

Alzheimer's disease is a progressive neurologic disorder that causes the brain to shrink (atrophy) and brain cells to die.

Find out more
Lewy Body Dementia

Protein deposits, called Lewy bodies, develop in nerve cells in the brain regions involved in thinking, memory and movement (motor control).

Find out more
Fronto-Temporal Dementia

Frontotemporal dementia is an umbrella term for a group of brain disorders that primarily affect the frontal and temporal lobes of the brain.

Find out more

Causes and Symptoms of Dementia

Depression

Simply put, chronic stress and/or depression may result in the release of cortisol.Over time, this stress hormone can harm the brain.

Alcohol

Alcoholism may increase the risk of certain medical conditions that damage the cardiovascular system, including high blood pressure.This thus causes dementia.

Drug Abuse

The researchers found that anticholinergic drugs in general were associated with a higher risk of dementia. More specifically, however, anticholinergic antidepressants, antipsychotic drugs, anti-Parkinson’s drugs, bladder drugs, and epilepsy drugs were associated with the highest increase in risk.

Memory loss

This is because dementia is caused by damage to the brain, and this damage can affect areas of the brain involved in creating and retrieving memories.

Difficulty communicating or finding words
  • Use of nonsense words
  • Repetition of questions
  • Fluent but inappropriate content
  • Unable to keep on the topic of conversation
Change in mood

One may be subject to mood swings and may display anger or aggression – seemingly for no apparent reason.
